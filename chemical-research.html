Pharma Industries



Helium is widely used in the pharmaceutical industry for various purposes. One of its primary uses is as a cooling agent in cryogenic applications, such as in the production of vaccines and medicines that require low-temperature storage. Helium is also used in gas chromatography, a technique used to separate and analyze different chemical compounds in pharmaceuticals.

Another important application of helium in the pharmaceutical industry is in magnetic resonance imaging (MRI). MRI machines use helium to cool down the magnets in the machine, which helps produce high-resolution images of the body's internal structures. Helium is also used in other medical imaging techniques, such as computed tomography (CT) and positron emission tomography (PET)

Other Applications

Helium is used in the packaging of certain pharmaceutical products, such as electronic monitoring devices that measure a patient's temperature, blood pressure, or glucose levels. These devices often contain helium-sealed sensors that require a controlled environment to function properly.


Overall, helium is a crucial component in the pharmaceutical industry and plays a vital role in ensuring the safety and efficacy of many medical products and technologies.
